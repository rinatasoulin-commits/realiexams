THE RISE OF URBAN FARMING

IIn many large cities around the world, people are beginning to grow food in unexpected places. Rooftops, balconies, and even empty parking areas are being used for what is known as urban farming. This growing trend has emerged1 as a response to rising food costs and environmental concerns.

IIUrban farming offers several important advantages. First, it allows people to produce fresh food locally instead of depending on long-distance transportation. In addition, it helps reduce pollution caused by trucks and factories. However, experts point out that limited space can restrict2 the amount of food that can be produced in cities.

IIIDespite these limitations, many communities have developed creative solutions. For example, some farmers use vertical systems that allow crops to grow in stacked layers. Others use advanced technology to monitor water and sunlight. These methods can increase3 production while using fewer resources.

IVUrban farming also has social benefits. It encourages cooperation among neighbors and helps build stronger communities. In some areas, local projects have been established to provide4 food for families in need. As a result, urban farming is not only a practical activity but also a meaningful social movement.

VExperts believe that urban farming will continue to expand in the future. Although it cannot completely replace traditional agriculture, it can contribute5 to food security and environmental protection. If cities continue to support these initiatives, urban farming may become an essential part of modern life.

GLOSSARY

  • 1. emerged — appeared / developed
  • 2. restrict — to limit
  • 3. increase — to make larger / to grow
  • 4. provide — to supply / give
  • 5. contribute — to add to / to help
