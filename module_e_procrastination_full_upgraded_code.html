Part I: Written Reception (70 points)

Answer questions 1–8 in English according to the article.

I Most people have experienced procrastination at some point in their lives. Students delay studying, employees postpone important tasks, and even simple daily responsibilities are often avoided. Although people usually believe procrastination is caused by laziness, researchers argue that it is more complex than it appears.
II One explanation is that procrastination is related to emotions rather than time management. When a task feels overwhelming or unpleasant, people tend to avoid it in order to reduce stress. In this way, procrastination becomes a short-term emotional relief, even though it creates problems in the long term.
III Another factor is fear of failure. Some individuals delay starting a task because they are afraid they will not succeed. By postponing the task, they protect themselves from possible disappointment. However, this behavior often leads to lower performance and increased anxiety.
IV Procrastination can also be influenced by a lack of clear goals. When people are unsure about what they need to do, they are more likely to delay action. Clear and specific goals can reduce uncertainty and encourage individuals to begin tasks earlier.
V Experts suggest breaking large tasks into smaller steps as a solution. This approach makes tasks feel more manageable and reduces the emotional pressure associated with them. Over time, this strategy can help people develop better work habits and improve productivity.
